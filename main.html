Coronary Angioplasty

Coronary Angioplasty, also known as percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI), is a minimally invasive procedure used to open clogged heart arteries. Angioplasty uses a tiny balloon catheter that is inserted in a blocked blood vessel to help widen it and improve blood flow to your heart.

Why it's done

Angioplasty is used to treat the buildup of fatty plaques in your heart's blood vessels. This buildup is a type of heart disease known as atherosclerosis. Angioplasty can be a treatment option if:

  • Medications or lifestyle changes haven't improved your heart health.
  • You have chest pain (angina) that is worsening.
  • You have a heart attack (Angioplasty can quickly open the blocked artery and reduce damage to your heart).

The Procedure and Recovery

During the procedure, a small wire mesh tube called a stent is often placed in the artery to keep it open and decrease the chance of it narrowing again. Most stents are coated with medication to help keep your artery open. Recovery is typically fast, with most patients returning home the next day and resuming normal activities within a week.

Transcatheter Aortic Valve Replacement (TAVR)

TAVR (Transcatheter Aortic Valve Replacement) is a groundbreaking, minimally invasive procedure designed to replace a narrowed aortic valve that fails to open properly (aortic valve stenosis) without the need for traditional open-heart surgery.

Who is a Candidate?

Originally approved for patients considered at high risk for open-heart surgery, TAVR is now increasingly an option for intermediate and even some low-risk patients. Our expert team conducts rigorous evaluations to determine if TAVR is the most appropriate and beneficial approach for your specific condition.

Benefits of TAVR

  • Minimally Invasive: Accessed via a small puncture in the groin, avoiding opening the chest.
  • Shorter Hospital Stay: Patients typically go home within a few days.
  • Faster Recovery: Quicker return to daily activities and an immediate improvement in quality of life.
  • Reduced Risk: Lower risk of severe complications compared to traditional open surgery for eligible patients.

Understanding Coronary Angioplasty: When Do You Need It?

Oct 12, 2026 4 min read

Coronary Angioplasty is a life-saving procedure designed to open clogged heart arteries.

What is Angioplasty?

It involves temporarily inserting and inflating a tiny balloon where your artery is clogged to help widen the artery. Often, a small wire mesh tube called a stent is placed to keep it open. This procedure immediately restores blood flow to the heart muscle.

Symptoms You Shouldn't Ignore

If you experience any of the following, seek medical evaluation immediately:

  • Unexplained chest pain (Angina), feeling like pressure or squeezing.
  • Shortness of breath during minimal exertion or at rest.
  • Radiating pain in the left arm, jaw, or back.
  • Unexplained fatigue accompanied by sweating or dizziness.
